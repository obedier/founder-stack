# [Product / Decision] — Press Release

**Headline:** [What we announce, in active voice. 8–12 words.]

**Sub-headline:** [The one-sentence "so what" for a busy reader.]

**Dateline:** [City], [absolute date 18 months from today]

---

[**Opening paragraph (4–6 sentences).** Announce the thing. Say what it is, who it's for, and why it exists. Write it as if it already shipped and is being covered by a real publication. No marketing prose, no hedging — concrete details only. If you have to write "powerful" or "next-generation," you don't understand it yet.]

> "[**Customer quote 1.** A real-sounding sentence from the customer whose problem this solved. Specific to their workflow, not generic. If you can't picture the customer saying this, the decision isn't ready.]"
> — [Customer role / company], [name optional]

> "[**Customer quote 2.** The alternative they would have lived with if this didn't exist. Names the pain in the customer's words.]"
> — [Customer role / company]

[**Closing paragraph.** What this enables next. What the team does Monday morning that they couldn't do before. End with the one number that matters (a metric, a unit economic, an adoption count) that proves it worked.]

## Internal FAQ (5 questions, answered)

### 1. Why now?

[What changed in the world that makes this the right moment. If "the world was always like this," the answer is no.]

### 2. Why us?

[What unfair advantage we have. If anyone could build this, why hasn't a better-positioned competitor done it already?]

### 3. What's actually hard about this?

[The 1–2 things that will determine success or failure. Not "execution" — be specific. A technical floor, an organizational shift, a regulatory bet.]

### 4. What does failure look like?

[Concretely. Not "we don't hit our numbers" — what visible signs of failure show up first, and when?]

### 5. What does the world look like 5 years out if this works?

[The compounding upside. Not the next product launch — the durable position this builds toward.]

## Self-check before convening the council

Before you fill in the voice positions, re-read the press release:

- [ ] Could you read this to a customer who already has the problem, and would they recognize it as a real solution to a real pain?
- [ ] Did you write the customer quote without using marketing language ("powerful," "seamless," "innovative")?
- [ ] Did you answer "what's hard about this" without saying "execution risk" or "scaling"?
- [ ] Is the closing-paragraph number a real metric that could be measured?

If three or more checks fail, **abort the council**. The question isn't ready. Refine the press release first.
